The Oracle

In the contemporary era, data holds the key to unveiling insights that may elude casual observation. This principle extends across various domains, including sports, such as football.

During my participation in Le Wagon's Data Analysis bootcamp, my team and I embarked on a challenge: to thoroughly examine the performance of a football team in order to identify its strengths and weaknesses. We pondered whether it was feasible, through data analysis, to predict the outcomes of their upcoming matches.

To achieve this, we chose to utilize data from the Italian Serie A. Immersed in the role of data analysts for the coaching staff of Inter Milan during the 2017-2018 season. The primary goal was to analyze the pivotal match scheduled for May 20, 2018: Inter vs. Lazio. This encounter would determine qualification for the fourth position and, consequently, the team's entry into the Champions League.

Our team's proposal involved the development of a Strategic Oracle that would provide a comprehensive solution based on advanced data analysis and predictive models to boost sporting success. The main features of the proposal were:

  1. Descriptive Analysis
  2. Outcome Prediction with Random Forest
  3. Heat Maps of On-Field Events and SWOT Analysis
  4. Final Outcome Prediction with Random Forest
  5. Comprehensive Predictive Dashboard

Descriptive Analysis

In the initial stage of analysis, the team delved into the simplest aspect of the data: the number of points each team had. It became evident that Inter could only advance to fourth place if they won the next match; a draw wouldn't be adequate to secure their spot in the Champions League.

After merging the tables and scrutinizing the data, several key insights emerged:

  • Victory rates: Initially, Inter had a strong start, while Lazio faced early setbacks but gradually improved. However, towards the end of the season, Lazio's victory rate surpassed that of Inter.
  • Match timing: Interestingly, both teams achieved more victories when matches were scheduled for evening hours.
  • Penalties: When confronted with penalty situations, Lazio demonstrated a greater propensity to capitalize on these opportunities, often swinging the match outcome in their favor. Moreover, it appeared that throughout the season, Lazio actively sought out penalty situations.
  • Fouls: In both scenarios, an increase in the number of fouls corresponded to a higher probability of losing the match.
  • Goals: Lazio scored more goals than Inter, but also conceded more goals.
